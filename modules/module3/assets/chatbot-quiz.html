1
What is the primary purpose of a chatbot in a legal practice setting?
A
To provide legal advice to clients automatically
B
To handle initial client inquiries and basic information gathering
C
To replace attorneys in routine legal tasks
D
To file legal documents with courts automatically
Correct Answer: B
Chatbots in legal practice should primarily handle initial client inquiries and basic information gathering, not provide legal advice or replace attorney functions. This ensures compliance with professional ethics rules.
2
Which of the following is a key advantage of no-code chatbot platforms for law firms?
A
They require extensive programming knowledge
B
They allow rapid deployment without technical expertise
C
They automatically ensure legal compliance
D
They eliminate all security concerns
Correct Answer: B
No-code platforms allow law firms to deploy chatbots quickly without requiring programming skills, making AI technology more accessible to legal professionals.
According to professional ethics rules, what must a law firm do when implementing a chatbot?
3
A
Nothing special - chatbots are exempt from ethics rules
B
Clearly disclose the chatbot's limitations and non-advisory nature
C
Only use chatbots for marketing purposes
D
Ensure the chatbot can provide legal advice
Correct Answer: B
Professional responsibility requires clear disclosure about chatbot limitations and ensuring clients understand they are not receiving legal advice from an AI system.
4
What is a major risk associated with chatbot conversations containing client information?
A
They might be too efficient
B
They could violate attorney-client privilege protections
C
They are always more expensive than human staff
D
They provide too much legal knowledge
Correct Answer: B
Chatbot conversations may not be protected under traditional attorney-client privilege, creating potential confidentiality risks that must be carefully managed.
5
Which ABA Model Rule is most directly relevant to chatbot implementation in law firms?
A
Rule 1.1 (Competence) - understanding the technology
B
Rule 3.1 (Meritorious Claims)
C
Rule 7.3 (Solicitation)
D
Rule 8.4 (Professional Responsibility)
Correct Answer: A
Rule 1.1 (Competence) requires attorneys to understand the technology they use, including AI chatbots, and their limitations and capabilities.
6
What should a chatbot do if a user asks for specific legal advice?
A
Provide the legal advice immediately
B
Refer the user to speak with a qualified attorney
C
Ignore the question completely
D
Provide a disclaimer and then give the advice
Correct Answer: B
Chatbots should redirect requests for legal advice to qualified attorneys to avoid unauthorized practice of law and ensure clients receive appropriate professional guidance.
7
When evaluating no-code chatbot platforms, which factor is MOST critical for law firms?
A
The lowest possible cost
B
The most advanced AI features
C
Security and compliance capabilities
D
Integration with social media platforms
Correct Answer: C
Security and compliance capabilities are most critical for law firms due to confidentiality requirements and professional responsibility obligations.
8
What is the best practice for ongoing chatbot management in a law firm?
A
Set up the chatbot once and let it run automatically
B
Regular monitoring, updates, and staff training
C
Only check the chatbot when problems are reported
D
Replace the chatbot annually with new technology
Correct Answer: B
Ongoing monitoring, regular updates, and continuous staff training ensure the chatbot remains compliant, effective, and aligned with firm policies.
