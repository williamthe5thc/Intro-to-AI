Module 1 Quiz: History of AI Knowledge Check

INSTRUCTIONS

This quiz tests your understanding of the key concepts covered in Module 1. You have 2 attempts to complete it, and you need a score of 80% or higher to pass.

Time Limit: 15 minutes
Number of Questions: 10
Question Types: Multiple choice, True/False, and Short answer

SAMPLE QUESTIONS

Question 1 (Multiple Choice)

When was the term "Artificial Intelligence" first coined at the Dartmouth Conference?

  • a) 1950
  • b) 1956
  • c) 1960
  • d) 1965

Question 2 (True/False)

The first AI winter occurred in the 1970s due to overpromising and underdelivering on AI capabilities.

Question 3 (Multiple Choice)

Which of the following best describes the primary difference between early AI systems and modern AI systems?

  • a) Early systems were faster
  • b) Modern systems use more electricity
  • c) Modern systems learn from data rather than following pre-programmed rules
  • d) Early systems were more accurate

Question 4 (Short Answer)

Name two factors that contributed to the AI boom of the 2010s and explain why each was important. (3-4 sentences)

Question 5 (Multiple Choice)

The Turing Test was designed to:

  • a) Measure computer processing speed
  • b) Test whether a machine could exhibit intelligent behavior equivalent to humans
  • c) Calculate the efficiency of algorithms
  • d) Determine the storage capacity of computers

Additional Questions Include:

  • Expert systems and their commercial applications
  • The role of internet-scale data in modern AI
  • Current generation AI tools and their era
  • Linear vs. cyclical nature of AI development
  • Natural language processing developments

Study Tips

  • Review the timeline of major AI milestones
  • Understand the causes and effects of AI winters
  • Know the key differences between rule-based and learning-based systems
  • Be familiar with important figures like Alan Turing
  • Understand how big data and computing power enabled modern AI

Feedback: After completing the quiz, you'll receive immediate feedback on each question along with explanations for the correct answers.
